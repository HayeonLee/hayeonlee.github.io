Intro

I'm a Postdoctoral Research Associate in the MURGe-Lab at the University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill, working with Prof. Mohit Bansal. Prior to joining UNC, I received my Ph.D. degree from Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST) in 2023, advised by Prof. Sung Ju Hwang, and won the best Ph.D. Dissertation Award from the KAIST College of Engineering and School of Computing. During my Ph.D. study, I interned at Microsoft Research, mentored by Dr. Yue Cao, and worked as a short-term visiting student at the Weizmann Institute of Science, hosted by Prof. Yonina Eldar. I received my B.S. and M.S. degrees from Ulsan National Institute of Science and Technology (UNIST).

For the long-term research goal, I've been focusing on tackling practical and real-world challenges to understand humans and impact our real lives through ever-evolving embodied AI systems with multiple agents & modalities.

My research interest includes the following topics:

  • Multimodal/Video Large Language Models: Comprehension, Generation, and Faithfulness
  • Compositional Generalization: Continual Learning and Compositional Reasoning
  • Efficient Training/Inference: Parameter-efficient Learning, Model Compression, and Federated Learning
