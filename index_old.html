Intro

I'm a Postdoctoral Research Associate in the MURGe-Lab at the University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill, working with Prof. Mohit Bansal. Prior to joining UNC, I received my Ph.D. degree from Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST) in 2023, advised by Prof. Sung Ju Hwang, and won the best Ph.D. Dissertation Award from the KAIST College of Engineering and School of Computing. During my Ph.D. study, I interned at Microsoft Research, mentored by Dr. Yue Cao, and worked as a short-term visiting student at the Weizmann Institute of Science, hosted by Prof. Yonina Eldar.

My research interest includes following topics:
  • Efficient Deep Learning: Continual Learning, Federated Learning, and Model Compression
  • Multimodal Learning: Egocentric Vision, Multimodal Video Understanding, and Generation
  • Learning with Real-world Data: Self-supervised Learning and Input Selective Learning

    • For my long-term research goal, I aim to encompass broad research fields to understand humans and impact our real lives through ever-evolving embodied AI systems with multiple agents & modalities. In particular, I've been focusing on tackling practical and real-world challenges in various research domains, including continual learning, multimodal learning (w/ audio, video, language, etc), online/streaming learning, and egocentric visions.
